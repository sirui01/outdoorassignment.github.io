Visitors Center

Over at the visitor center, visitors can learn more about how to locate themselves through the wetland reserve.
They can also visit the gallery, where it features some of the popular organisms found in the wetland reserve. Visitors can learn more about the history of the reserve at the gallery before they start their journey for discovery!

Junior Adventure Trail

This feature for children includes prop roots and small platforms raised above the mud, so they can experience what is like to be a crab or mudskipper living among the mangroves. Children under the age of 13 must be accompanied and supervised by adults while exploring the trail.

Mid Canopy Walk

This suspended bridge runs through the understory of a secondary forest in the reserve. If you look carefully you may be able to spot birds like pied fantails and insects such as cicadas that forage and reside in the forest.

Coastal Trail

This route offers a scenic and uninterrupted view of the Kranji waterfront, where you can even see some kelongs in Malaysian waters. A lookout point along the way is a good place to observe ospreys and white-bellied sea eagles hunting for prey.

Mud Experience

During low tide, visitors can step onto the mudflats and get up to close to creatures living in the mud. It features a balancing bridge which lowers visitors down to a platform near the bottom of the mudflats. Spot mudskippers hunting for food or basking in the sun during low tide!

Mangrove Broadwalk

The Mangrove Boardwalk also makes for a great introduction to the Mangrove Forest, an inter-tidal area and a barrier between land and sea. Get up close and personal with the wildlife and mangrove trees without getting your shoes wet or muddy! Catch glimpses of native inhabitants such as mudskippers, otters, crabs, water snakes and more!
